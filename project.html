The recovery program.

It will be no easy task creating the perfect recovery, education and resettlement program. There are just so many different pieces of this puzzle to fit together. The primary hurdles identified: problem identification, idea generation, voting and reaching conclusions. Our aim is to utalise a system that functions without authority and that can scale to any size of community. The system must also encourage speed, so that ideas can arise, be reviewed, voted upon and implemented as quickly as possible.


It is our intention to meet these requirements by combining two different systems. A community collaboration platform such as Discourse and a voting mechanism.

Without Authority?

Being community driven, it is our belief that having a board of advisors or any centralised location for filtering ideas will be detrimental to the overall scalability and robustness of Project Phoenix. It would also tie up resources, time and conflicts with our ethos of transparency and egalitarianism.


Possible System - IBDD

As mentioned, we need a means of idea generation that encourages people to specialise around their field of knowledge and that weighs ideas and respective criticisms without authority. Enter Issue Based Direct Democracy.

Created by Max Kaye, IBDD utalises blockchain to ensure transparent and tamper proof voting. It also utalises some psychological and physical restrictions from economics by using non-fungible tokens for every action within the system.


Two Tokens

Liquidity tokens and Voting tokens. Neither are 'real' money and they are tradeable with each other (rate depending on supply and demand).These tokens exist purely to allow conjecture and criticism to be weighed without authority.

Upon joining the community you will be given a sum of Liquidity tokens, say 1000. Suggesting new legislation (proposed actionable change) costs Liquidity tokens. Every time new legislation is posted you receive 1 vote token, which by default if you don't use goes up for auction. To defend unfavourable legislation would require an individual to drain their Liquidity tokens, but this wouldn't go far.


Tokens are constantly redistributed to ensure an egalitarian system. This model makes pushing bad ideas economically infeasible and encourages specialisation around expertise.

The end result is that we have an ecosystem where anyone can suggest ideas and the community decides if this is a good fit. Project Phoenix will use the ideas generated from this system in our recovery program.

How we fund Phoenix

Project Phoenix is funded by everyday people who invest in the project by purchasing FIRE tokens. FIRE tokens are security tokens that live on the Ethereum blockchain. Owning FIRE tokens makes you a co-owner of Project Phoenix and as Phoenix grows and matures, so too does the value of your FIRE tokens.
