Problem

Whilst attempting to reduce and prevent addiction, the perfect system for keeping people addicted was discovered. This system demonises addicts, segregates them from our communities and forces them into poverty, disease and crime. This system makes reintroduction into society almost impossible. There is no hope for most addicts to ever contribute to society again and that's not their fault. We can fix this.

Any solution must:

1

Create an economic incentive for communities to shift from oppressing addicts to wanting them to succeed.

2

Consult experts to make a scientifically sound yet revolutionary program to make addicts reconnect with life.

3

Be adaptable to any community from Vancouver in Canada to Tokyo in Japan.

Introducing Project Phoenix


Project Phoenix has created a model where communities all around the world can create and fund programs to get addicts integrated back into society.

The New Incentive

We have created a link between the addicts success and the communities who fund them. Individuals will be able to purchase FIRE tokens to support Project Phoenix. These represent ownership in the organisation. As addicts return to work, they repay back into Project Phoenix - like a student loan. This increases the value of FIRE tokens, meaning individuals will now benefit financially by supporting addicts.



Expert Created Program

To give addicts and supporters the greatest chance of success we have created a method for experts from all over the world to collaborate and create the perfect programs. This means that Doctors, Lawyers, Psychologists and many other professions are no longer limited by their geographic boundaries. We can seek the most up to date and effective knowledge.



Adaptable To Communities

The specific needs of different locations will be different therefore, each facility and related network will be different. Each center will receive the same support and knowledge from Project Phoenix and our supporting community but will have the freedom to adapt to their own individual needs.
